Bird of Paradise

Strelitzia reginae

A Bird of Paradise is a flower native to South Africa. Also known as Strelitzia or its species specification Strelitzia reginae, it is a genus of five species of prennial plant, beloning to the plant family of Strelitziaceae.

It is typically a garden plant, best thriving in tropical or subtropical environments. As it is a flower that is easily grown and does not take much to maintain, it is also grown as an indoor plant for people living in more cooler climates.

Being a beloved flower, it is featured on the reverse of the South African 50 cent coin and this distinct flower is also known far beyond its country of origin, with the bird of paradise flower being the floral emblem of the city of Los Angeles.

Species and hybrids

There are five species that are recognised, however the S. juncea has been showed to be genetically nested with S. reginae that is possibly a mutation that is in the process of speciating.

  • Strelitzia alba aka White bird of paradise
  • Strelitzia caudata aka Mountain strelitzia
  • Strelitzia nicolai aka White bird of paradise/giant bird of paradise/wild banana/blue and white strelitzia

  • Strelitzia reginae aka Strelitzia aka Bird of Paradise or crane lily

  • Strelitzia juncea aka African desert banana
